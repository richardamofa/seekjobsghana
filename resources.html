Resume-Tips

Top Resume Tips for Job Seekers

  1. Keep It One Page (Max Two if Needed)

    2. Focus on the most recent, relevant, and impressive experiences. Recruiters skim!

  2. Tailor for Each Job

    3. Customize your resume to match the job description. Use keywords from the posting.

  3. Start with a Strong Summary

    4. Write 2–3 lines at the top that highlight who you are, your key skills, and what you’re seeking.

  4. Highlight Achievements, Not Just Duties

    5. Use bullet points that show results.
    Instead of: “Managed social media”
    Try: “Increased Instagram engagement by 40% in 3 months”

  5. Education + Certifications

    6. List your degree, school, and graduation year. Add relevant certificates (e.g. AWS, Google, etc.)

  6. Proofread Everything

    7. One typo can cost you. Double-check spelling and grammar.

Remeber these top tips for interview

  1. Know the Role & the Company

    2. i. Review the job description and align your skills.

    ii. Learn 2–3 quick facts about the company (values, products, mission).

  2. Prep 2–3 STAR Stories

    3. i. Have examples ready using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

    ii. Use them for common questions like:

    • “Tell me about yourself”
    • “A challenge you overcame”
    • “A time you worked in a team”

  3. Practice the Must-Know Questions

    • Why should we hire you?
    • What are your strengths/weaknesses?
    • Why do you want this job?

  4. Show Up Sharp

    • Dress well, arrive early (10–15 mins), and stay calm.
    • Smile, make eye contact, and bring good energy.

  5. End Strong & Ask Smart Questions

    • Ask 1–2 thoughtful questions like:

      “What does success look like in this role?”

    • Send a thank-you email afterward (brief and professional).

Networking: Your Secret Career Weapon

In today’s job market, it’s not just what you know — it’s who knows you. Networking opens hidden doors to opportunities that never make it to job boards. From building genuine connections on LinkedIn to attending local meetups or virtual events, smart networking lets your name travel where your résumé can’t. Approach every interaction with curiosity, value, and follow-up — and you'll turn conversations into career moves.

Try These Simple Strategies:

Optimize your SeekJobs profile

Make it clear, complete, and active.

Join industry groups

Online communities or local meetups.

Engage meaningfully

Comment, share insights, or ask thoughtful questions.

Follow up

After a chat or event, send a short thank-you or connection message.

Offer value

Share helpful info or make introductions when you can.

Stay consistent

Networking works over time, not overnight.

Build Bridges, Not Just Résumés

Grow Connections, Grow Careers

Networking isn’t about asking for jobs — it’s about building relationships that lead to opportunities. Whether online or in person, start by connecting with people in your field, sharing your interests, and staying in touch. The right connection can open the right door.

Career Development

Career Development: Grow with Intention

Your career is more than a job — it’s a journey. Whether you're starting out or stepping up, developing your skills, setting goals, and seeking feedback are key to long-term success. Growth doesn’t just happen — it’s built.

Quick Ways to Fuel Your Growth:

Set clear goals

Short- and long-term. Define what you want. Clear goals give your career direction and drive.

Keep learning

Stay sharp. Take courses, read, and build skills that match your path.

Seek mentorship

Learn from those ahead. Mentors guide, inspire, and open new doors.

Track your progress

Reflect often. Celebrate wins and use setbacks to learn and adjust.

Be adaptable

Stay flexible. The world changes fast — grow with it, not against it.

Stay Consistent

Growth takes time. Show up, stay committed, and keep pushing — even when it’s slow.

Here are some frequently asked questions in the community

FAQ's

  1. How do I write a strong résumé?

    2. - Keep it simple, clear, and tailored to the job. Highlight relevant skills and results, not just tasks.

  2. What should I include in a cover letter?

    3. - Show why you're the right fit. Mention the role, your interest in the company, and how your experience matches the job.

  3. How can I prepare for an interview?

    4. - Research the company, practice common questions, and prepare stories using the STAR method.

  4. What’s the best way to network as a beginner?

    5. - Start by connecting with people on LinkedIn, joining industry groups, and reaching out with a short, friendly message

  5. Should I apply even if I don’t meet all the job requirements?

    6. - Yes! If you meet most and are eager to learn, go for it. Many employers hire for potential, not perfection.
